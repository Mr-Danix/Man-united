MANCHESTER UNITED

The most successful football club in English history with 20 league titles, 12 FA Cups, and 3 European Cups. Founded in 1878 as Newton Heath LYR Football Club, we became Manchester United in 1902 and have been dominating English and European football ever since.

CLUB HISTORY

1878
Newton Heath LYR Football Club was formed by the Carriage and Wagon department of the Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway depot.
1902
The club was renamed Manchester United after financial difficulties threatened its existence.
1958
The Munich air disaster claimed the lives of 8 Manchester United players, a tragedy that shook the football world.
1968
Manchester United became the first English club to win the European Cup, defeating Benfica 4-1 at Wembley.
1999
The Treble-winning season under Sir Alex Ferguson: Premier League, FA Cup, and UEFA Champions League.
2008
United won their third European Cup, defeating Chelsea in a dramatic penalty shootout in Moscow.

OLD TRAFFORD

Known as the "Theatre of Dreams," Old Trafford has been Manchester United's home since 1910. With a capacity of over 74,000, it's the largest club football stadium in the United Kingdom and the second-largest overall after Wembley.

The stadium has witnessed countless historic moments, from Sir Bobby Charlton's record-breaking goals to Cristiano Ronaldo's spectacular performances.
